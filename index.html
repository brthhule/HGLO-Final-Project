HGLO 101: Mosquitos and Oil

Exploring the parallel developments of Mosquito Eradication via Genetic Modification and American External Oil Dependency

By Brennen Le

Featured Readings

Learn more about Genetic Modification

Summary

  • For an estimated 190-226 million years, mosquitos have plagued the Earth
  • During that time, they have killed 52 billion people via communicable diseases (such as Malaria)
  • It is estimated that there have been 108 billion to ever live in human existence
  • This means that mosquitos have killed close to half of all humans that have existed
  • For most of human history, we have lived in fear of the mosquito and its deadly bites
  • However, tides are changing as researchers develop genetic modification technologies to decrease the reproduction of malaria-carrying mosquitos

Learn more about American Oil Reliance

Summary

  • Access to oil is inherently tied to nations' securities-- More oil means that a nation can better defend itself
  • For a long time, the most efficient way to get oil was to go to oil deposits (going to other countries and spending resources there)
  • As such, during the Cold War, the U.S. expanded its oil network through satelite states
  • The resulting network has resulted in a vast, global, interconnected oil market
  • However, after the rise of fracking, the U.S. saw a decreased need to rely on foreign oil
  • As such, we are entering a new age wherein the U.S. sources most of its oil internally
